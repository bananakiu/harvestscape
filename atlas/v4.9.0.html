The Story

Willowbrook was once bound together by the Guild of Nine Crafts — nine wings, nine trades, and one night a year when the whole valley gathered on the coast under floating lanterns. Then Rosa Alderman died, her closest friend — your grandfather Aldous, the Festival-Keeper — let the lanterns go dark, and the valley began to forget how to be a valley. The Guild closed one craft at a time. Maya's father walked north and didn't come back. Decades later, Grandpa leaves you the farm — and, without quite saying so at first, the one thing he couldn't do himself.

Act I — wake the valley. Prove the crafts still live (farm, chop, mine, fish, cook), light the Guild's wings, recover the Star Metal from the sealed vault, and bring the Grand Festival back to the coast. The finale plays as a full festival-night cutscene and ends the main chain — but not the story.

Act II — the empty chair. After the lanterns, Rowan asks a harder, kinder thing: the truth about Elias Alderman, eleven years up the coast road, and whether Maya's father can be brought home. It ends with two names carved on a standing stone on your own farm.

And after: the festival becomes an annual fixture (the anniversary of your night), the almanac pages keep arriving through the long middle of the game, and the last page — found only after everything else — asks you to do the one thing Grandpa never did: write to your mother.

My dear grandchild,

If you're reading this, the old farm is yours now. I know it doesn't look like much — the weeds have had their way, and the valley's gone quiet since the Guild closed its doors.

But this soil remembers every seed I ever planted. Tend it, and it'll tend to you. Plant a little each day, water what you sow, and rest when the sun goes down.

I'll not dress up the rest: Willowbrook is fading. The Guild of Nine Crafts stands dark, one craft at a time gone cold, and the Grand Festival that once lit the whole coast hasn't been held in years. I always meant to wake it back up — relight the crafts, put the lanterns back on the water. I ran out of seasons. Maybe you won't.

The rest, you'll figure out — same as I did. Oh — and do say hello to Maya. She's good people.

My dear grandchild,

If you've opened this chest, then you've made the old place a home again — which means you're ready for the rest of it.

I was the valley's Festival-Keeper, once. The ninth craft of the Guild was never a trade you could sell at Tom's counter. It was the gathering — lanterns on the water, one long table, the whole valley in one place for one night. I kept that flame.

Then one winter we lost Rosa Alderman — Maya's grandmother, the finest baker Willowbrook ever had, and the truest friend I had in this world. I couldn't light the lanterns without her. 'Next year,' I said. I said it for a great many years.

A valley that forgets to gather forgets how to be a valley. The Guild's doors closed one craft at a time — and it began with mine.

The pin was Rosa's, then mine. It's yours now. I broke a thing by grieving too long, kiddo. You'll mend it by living well.

— Grandpa

I'm not there tonight — but I know exactly how it looks, because I saw it a hundred times before I let it go dark. The lanterns doubling on the black water. The Aldermans laughing. Tom giving away more than he sells. Some child eating far too much.

My dear grandchild,

There is one thing I have not written down anywhere, and I find I can only write it on the last page of a book nobody was ever meant to finish.

Your mother left this valley at nineteen and she was right to. I told her so at the gate, and then I went inside and did not come out for two days.

She wrote every month. I answered perhaps one letter in three, because I am a coward with a pen, and because every letter I did not answer was a small proof that I was still angry about something I could not name.

The World

One valley, 16 places. Only the farm persists between days — interiors, the mine, and the coast regenerate each morning, which is why the mine can be endless and the beach can be storm-strewn. Late-game gold buys back three pieces of the old valley (Rowan's ledger), which then exist in the world forever.

Willowbrook Farm outdoor · 46×36

Where you wake on day 1 — and since v3, purely a farm: fields, both ponds, the meadow, the southwest woods. The east road leads to the village; the Grove path west.

On the village plaza. Maya's home (and later, Elias's tea by the pond).

Guild of Nine Crafts · once, the heart of the valley interior · 17×11

North of the village plaza. Elder Rowan keeps the nine dark wings; the story's heart.

The Undercroft · the tenth wing interior · 24×16

Behind the planked tenth door in the Guild, opened by 'The Tenth Door' (Act III; needs Act II done + total level 100). The Warden's wing, sealed eleven years — floors 1–30 of cozy-dark cavern, knotted with restless things you SETTLE with the Stave (never kill; nothing is ever taken, knockout costs zero). Five families band by depth (Gloam Wisp → Knot-Shambler → Ember Mite → Hollow Warden, which guards its front → Gloam Tangle, which splits), and every 10th floor a Great Knot boss guards the descent. The way down hides under a knot (or the boss) on each floor; a Warden's Bell every 5th floor is a Pledge-Ledger checkpoint you can ring back down to. Time stands still, like the mine. The only hazardous space in the valley — and only because you walk into it.

The Old Mine interior · 24×16

The village's north ridge. Endless floors, regenerated daily. The Old Lift beside each floor's ladder rides to the surface free; every 5th floor's stop can be restored (wood + ore + gold) to skip down forever. Time stands still underground. The sealed vault waits below (Mining 20).

Willowbrook Coast · salt on the breeze outdoor · 46×24

The village's south path. Bram's coast — salmon water, shore forage, festivals, and every finale.

The Coast Road · north, by the sea outdoor · 46×26

East along the shore from the coast (v3.36). The Gullwater river and its estuary — river fishing's home water — the plank ford, roadside forage (samphire, sea holly), and the old ferry landing where the milestone reads MARROW POINT — 39. Elias walks up every fourth day. The road runs on north; the map never will.

Starfall Ridge · where the sky came down outdoor · 46×30

Up the switchbacks past the mine mouth (v3.43). The mountain above the mine: alpine forage on the scree (mountain thyme, snowdrops), the crater dell where the Guild's star fell, a wind-worn bench, and the cairn that opens the panorama of the whole valley. On clear nights the summit catches Starlight Shards — gleanable after dusk, with a rare splinter of true star metal among them.

Butterbrook · the coast dairy outdoor · 46×34

West off the beach, the coast opens south (v3.44). Shore-meadows, the brook to the sea under a plank footbridge, and the creamery at the far west — the longest walk in the valley. Home of Nell, the coast dairy keeper (Tom's wife), who buys dairy goods — milk, cheese, wool, eggs — at a daily premium order, closing the barn→coast→cheese loop. Samphire forages on the sand.

The Coast Dairy · cool stone, cream, and patience interior · 13×9

Inside the Butterbrook creamery. Nell works here by day (the meadow of an evening). Talk to her to fill today's dairy order for coin, Farming XP, and her regard.
